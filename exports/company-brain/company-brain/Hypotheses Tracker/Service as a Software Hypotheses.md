The “Service as a Software” market will likely organize around a core tension:
vendors absorbing operational complexity vs customers retaining it.
Companies that move closer to outcome ownership (not tooling) may capture disproportionate value over pure SaaS players.
The market may consolidate into a few dominant models:
infrastructure/orchestration layers
vertical autonomous “digital employees”
managed AI-native service providers
Trust, governance, and observability layers will become primary scaling bottlenecks for autonomy.
Hybrid models (software + hidden operations) may outperform pure software in early enterprise adoption.

- Manufacturing procurement (direct materials procurement) is a more defensible wedge than generic procurement automation.
- Exception handling (delays, shortages, mismatches) may be the highest-value initial automation surface.
- Mid-market manufacturing companies (1,000–5,000 employees) are the best ICP due to high pain + low automation maturity.
- Procurement outsourcing demand signals that companies are already willing to externalize parts of procurement operations, enabling “procurement-as-a-service” evolution.
- Greenfield manufacturing or newly expanding operations may adopt AI-native procurement faster than legacy enterprises.
- “Control tower / intelligence layer” approaches may have faster adoption than full execution replacement systems.
