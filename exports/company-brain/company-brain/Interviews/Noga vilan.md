## סיכום


## Key Points

- Naama introduced the startup idea: an AI-native procurement solution focused mainly on indirect procurement and procurement process optimization.
- Noga shared that she recently attended a procurement innovation conference where most solutions were focused on indirect procurement for smaller or less mature companies that are only now formalizing procurement processes beyond credit card spending.
- Noga described Vestas as a very large, global, and highly legacy procurement organization with significant operational complexity.
- She explained that one of the biggest challenges is lack of visibility and orchestration across a very large procurement organization — understanding who is needed, who owns what, and where bottlenecks exist.
- The company already uses Anthropic Claude, but mainly as a reactive tool. Employees still need to manually provide context every time, and the AI does not understand the organization’s workflows, history, or operational processes.
- Noga described several AI use cases she would find valuable:
    - Feasibility analysis for new projects
    - Price comparisons and market analysis
    - Fuel / market condition analysis
    - Recommendations based on previous organizational experience
    - AI that understands both company history and external market conditions
- A major theme in the conversation was the need for AI systems to have organizational memory and proactive operational understanding, not just chatbot capabilities.
- Naama introduced the idea of “procurement as a service” — outsourcing an entire procurement workflow/process to a third party that uses AI internally but still provides a human accountable owner.
- Noga responded positively to the accountability aspect and emphasized that she does not mind outsourcing as long as there is a clearly responsible person/team she can hold accountable.
- Noga expressed relatively high openness toward AI adoption and workforce reduction enabled by AI compared to more conservative enterprise buyers.
- At the same time, she strongly implied that Vestas is probably not the ideal first customer due to:
    - organizational complexity,
    - legacy systems,
    - scale,
    - integration difficulty,
    - and operational overhead.
- She also suggested that a forward deployed engineer / highly customized embedded implementation approach would likely not fit an organization like hers.

## Conclusions


### **Pains**

- Lack of visibility across large procurement organizations
- Difficulty orchestrating complex procurement workflows and ownership
- AI tools lack organizational context and historical memory
- Existing AI tools are reactive rather than proactive
- Legacy enterprise environments make adoption and change management difficult
- Integrating new procurement tools into mature organizations is operationally heavy
- Procurement knowledge is fragmented across systems and people

---


### **Objections**

- Enterprise organizations are difficult to penetrate due to legacy infrastructure and complexity
- Concerns around accountability and ownership when introducing AI into procurement processes
- Skepticism toward adding another standalone tool into an already complex environment
- Highly customized / forward-deployed implementation models may not scale operationally
- Fear that some AI procurement startups are effectively consulting services disguised as software

---


### **Buying Signals**

- Strong interest in AI solutions that understand organizational workflows and historical context
- Clear openness to AI-assisted procurement decision making
- Interest in proactive AI recommendations rather than simple copilots/chatbots
- Positive response to the “human accountability layer” within procurement-as-a-service
- Interest in operational visibility and orchestration tools
- Willingness to outsource procurement workflows if accountability and service quality are guaranteed
- Shared concrete AI use cases and operational gaps, indicating active problem awareness

---


### **Fit / Not-Fit Hints**


### Potential Fit

- Enterprise procurement orchestration and visibility
- AI systems with organizational memory/context
- Managed procurement services with strong accountability
- Narrow, process-specific workflow automation
- AI-assisted feasibility and sourcing intelligence

### Not a Good Fit

- Generic SMB indirect procurement tools
- Simple procurement chatbots/copilots
- Highly horizontal “AI procurement platform” positioning
- Heavy forward-deployed engineering models for large enterprises
- Attempting to replace entire procurement organizations end-to-end from day one
- Starting with highly complex global enterprises like Vestas as first customers
