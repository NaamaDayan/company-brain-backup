This was another major idea we explored deeply.


Original thesis:


Meetings are one of the largest coordination surfaces inside organizations. Important context, decisions, and follow-ups constantly disappear after meetings.


The idea was not just to build a meeting summarizer, but an internal meeting agent that could:

- Understand organizational context.
- Track decisions over time.
- Drive execution after meetings.
- Connect conversations across teams.
- Automatically coordinate follow-ups and workflows.

Why we stopped pursuing it:

- The market became extremely crowded very quickly.
- Meeting summaries became commoditized.
- Most products converged toward similar feature sets.
- It was difficult to create a defensible wedge beyond note-taking.
- The jump from summarization → execution turned out to be operationally harder than expected.

Key learning:

- Users care much more about execution than summaries.
- Capturing information is not enough; software must drive outcomes.
- AI products become weak very quickly when they stop at passive insight generation.
- Distribution and workflow embedding matter more than model quality alone.

Current conviction:


We still believe conversational interfaces and meeting intelligence will become part of enterprise infrastructure, but we no longer believe standalone meeting agents are the strongest company opportunity.
