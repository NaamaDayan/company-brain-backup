This was one of the more serious ideas we explored and spent meaningful time thinking about.


Original thesis:


The internal side of enterprise sales is extremely operational. A large percentage of the work is not customer-facing selling, but internal coordination:

- Preparing account context.
- Driving internal alignment.
- Coordinating stakeholders.
- Tracking blockers.
- Managing follow-ups.
- Updating systems.
- Moving deals through organizational processes.

We believed there was an opportunity to build an AI-native internal operating layer for sales teams.


Why we became less convinced:

- Many workflows were too organization-specific.
- The ROI was difficult to demonstrate quickly.
- Sales teams already have extremely crowded tooling environments.
- It was difficult to identify a narrow wedge with immediate urgency.
- A large portion of the value depended on deep integrations and organizational behavior change.

What we learned:

- Internal operational pain is often more severe than external customer-facing pain.
- Buyers respond strongly when software removes coordination overhead.
- AI products that require broad organizational behavior changes have very slow adoption cycles.
- The best AI workflow products usually start with one painful operational bottleneck instead of trying to orchestrate an entire function.

Current view:


We still believe there is a real long-term opportunity around internal operational systems for revenue teams, but we no longer believe the initial scope we explored was the right starting point.
