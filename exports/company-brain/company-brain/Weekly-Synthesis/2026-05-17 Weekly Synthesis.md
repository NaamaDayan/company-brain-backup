## 📉 Beliefs That Changed This Week


**Old belief: 'Agent lifecycle / HR system for agents / organizational brain for agents' is the company's primary direction.**

- _What changed:_ Ran-2 reasoning (May 14) plus Omer Sella interview (May 14) surfaced that (a) the buyer is governance/IAM/security — not HR/COO; (b) the broader category will be absorbed by Microsoft, OpenAI, ServiceNow, and security platforms; (c) the pre-deployment governance moment is unoccupied but lacks WTP clarity as a horizontal play.
- _New belief:_ Service-as-Software in a chosen vertical is the company's primary direction. 'Company Brain' may remain a defensible component if wedged into a vertical (e.g., 'company brain for insurance GTM teams'), but not as a horizontal product.
- _Why conviction shifted:_ The horizontal agent layer has too many well-capitalized incumbents in motion. The defensibility argument requires verticalization. This decision is logged with High confidence in the Decision Log.

**Old belief: 'Procurement is the obvious next vertical.'**

- _What changed:_ Ran-1 (May 14) and Ran-5 (May 14) introduced a structural objection: Sequoia's framework classifies mid-market procurement as INSOURCED, weakening the BPO-displacement playbook. Ran-5 specifically: 'do NOT treat general AI procurement workforce as a blue-ocean opportunity.'
- _New belief:_ **Open question.** Procurement is still on the table but only with a sharply narrower wedge (mid-market PaaS, post-contract enforcement, SaaS renewals, or vertical-regulated procurement), AND only after live discovery proves Naama-4's mid-market thesis over Ran-1's insourced objection.
- _Why conviction shifted:_ When two of our own research artifacts contradict each other on a foundational point, the responsible move is to suspend conviction until customer data resolves it. Both arguments are well-evidenced; we cannot pick a side from desk research.

## ⚡ Contradictions


**Contradiction 1: Mid-market procurement opportunity.**

- _Source A:_ Naama-4 (2026-05-15) — 'open white space is specifically AI-operated PaaS for US mid-market.'
- _Source B:_ Ran-1 (2026-05-14) + Ran-5 (2026-05-14) — Sequoia classifies mid-market procurement as insourced; building a general 'Lio for procurement' is not attractive.
- _Recency verdict:_ Naama-4 is one day newer, but the contradiction is substantive not chronological — both are well-reasoned with sources. Recency does not break this tie.
- _Possible explanation:_ Both are correct depending on the wedge. 'PaaS for mid-market' framed as **BPO displacement** is weak (no BPO to displace). Framed as **headcount avoidance** ('you'll never need to hire a Head of Procurement,' selling to CFO not CPO) is stronger and consistent with both views.
- _How to resolve:_ 5–10 customer discovery calls with VP Ops / CFOs at $20M–$100M US companies. Specifically ask: 'How are you handling procurement today? Are you about to hire? What does spend management look like in practice?' (Decision logged.)

**Contradiction 2: Adoption of existing forecasting/sales tools.**

- _Source A:_ Matt Casatelli (DigiCert, 2026-05-14) — forecasting tools exist with 30-day staleness alerts 'but I just don't know how well people are using them.'
- _Source B:_ Fernando Castellanos (everC, 2026-05-14) — 'Clari does a really good visual dashboard, high level, fairly accurate' and is actively used.
- _Recency verdict:_ Same day, both real CROs. Likely a company-size / sales-management-culture difference (DigiCert is much larger and more diffuse than everC).
- _How to resolve:_ In future DEMI discovery calls, segment by company size and sales-manager tech-adoption profile (the '70% relationship-oriented / 30% tech-oriented' split Matt described) before assuming pain shape.

## 🧭 Strategic Implications

1. **The motion is decided. The vertical is not.** Service-as-software / autopilot is the only thesis with multi-source agreement. Therefore: stop debating motion, focus debate on vertical selection.
2. **Two viable thesis paths remain — Procurement (Naama) and Sales Execution Memory / DEMI (interview-validated).** Both deserve 2 weeks of structured customer discovery; both have a clear validation plan; neither should be deepened further by desk research until customer data arrives. _Therefore:_ allocate the next 14 days to talking to people, not reading more.
3. **Pricing should be outcome-based from day one in whichever vertical we pick.** _Therefore:_ every pricing conversation in the next round of discovery should explicitly probe whether the buyer would prefer pay-per-cure / % of savings vs. annual SaaS contract.
4. **Distribution is the bottleneck, not problem validity.** Fernando's '100 pitches/week, can't differentiate' is the harshest constraint we have. _Therefore:_ any vertical we choose must come with a non-cold channel (CRO community, PE fund relationship, regulated-industry domain network). No cold outbound thesis survives this constraint.
5. **Without a vertical-domain co-founder, no chosen vertical works.** _Therefore:_ recruiting that person is on the critical path — not a parallel workstream.
