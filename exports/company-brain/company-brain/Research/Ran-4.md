## Key Insights

- Microsoft’s Work Trend Index frames the next AI challenge as **organizational absorption**, not individual adoption: companies need to redesign work, governance, management practices, and learning loops around AI.
- The core problem identified: employees already use AI unevenly; stronger employees develop better patterns, prompts, context habits, and correction behaviors, but these remain personal and are not turned into shared departmental capability.
- “What was learned” cannot be inferred from AI usage alone. It must be tied to a defined workflow, approved output, human corrections, and ideally business/workflow outcomes.
- A passive skill/prompt library is likely weak because employees must know it exists, know what to search for, and remember to use it.
- The stronger product direction is **proactive in-flow skill injection**: the system detects a relevant work moment and surfaces the right skill/checklist/template automatically.
- The least privacy-sensitive starting point is not desktop/browser monitoring, but **learning from approved high-quality artifacts**: proposals, follow-ups, QBRs, procurement briefs, escalation responses, etc.
- Browser/desktop extension is productively powerful but privacy-sensitive. A safer path is:
    1. approved artifacts,
    2. workflow/event triggers,
    3. Slack/Teams/CRM/Docs nudges,
    4. only later, whitelisted browser extension if needed.
- Milestone/mstone.ai is a strong adjacent signal: it does something similar for engineering productivity and GenAI coding impact, but not for business departments or real-time skill injection.

## Conclusions

- The problem is real, but it should not be framed as “AI learning” or “prompt management.” The sharper framing is:

    **organizations don’t know what good AI-assisted work looks like inside each department, which patterns improve performance, or how to scale them at the moment of work.**

- The passive version is probably nice-to-have. The proactive version has stronger potential because it connects directly to performance, consistency, onboarding, and workflow quality.
- The best initial wedge is likely a narrow, repeated, outcome-linked workflow, especially in **Sales / RevOps / Sales Enablement**, such as proposals, follow-ups, discovery prep, or deal execution.
- The main differentiation must be:

    **business-department skills learned from real approved work, then injected in-flow**, not generic prompt libraries, dashboards, or adoption analytics.

- Privacy and trust are central. The product must be positioned as learning from approved work patterns, not monitoring employees.

## Hypotheses

- Organizations that bought Copilot/ChatGPT Enterprise are increasingly asking: “Who is using AI well, what are they doing differently, and how do we scale that?”
- High-performing employees leave recognizable patterns in approved artifacts and edits that can be converted into reusable skills.
- Workflow-event triggers may be enough for early proactive injection without invasive browser monitoring.
- Sales teams may show higher urgency and WTP than Product, HR, or general knowledge-work teams because outputs are repeated and tied to revenue.
- A “departmental skill graph” built from task type → context → artifact → correction → approved pattern → outcome could become a defensible moat.
- Buyers will respond better to ROI/performance framing than to enablement/training framing.
- Existing tools solve adjacent problems — adoption analytics, DAPs, prompt libraries, sales enablement, AI governance — but do not fully solve skill mining + proactive injection for business departments.

## Decisions

- The problem hypothesis was refined to:

    **AI usage is growing, but organizations fail to identify, standardize, and deliver the best AI-assisted work patterns to employees in real time.**

- The preferred MVP direction is:

    **start from approved high-quality artifacts and workflow events**, not broad browser/desktop monitoring.

- Browser extension, if used, should be limited to **whitelisted enterprise apps/pages** and introduced only after value is proven.
- The solution should be framed around **proactive skill injection**, not a passive prompt/skill library.
- Milestone was identified as an adjacent validation and warning: validation that AI-impact measurement matters, warning that a pure dashboard/analytics product is not differentiated enough.
