## Key Insights

- **AI-native** was defined as redesigning work around AI, not adding AI as a tool on top of existing workflows.
- A good AI-native candidate is a department where much of the work can shift from **human-executed** to **AI-executed / AI-orchestrated / human-approved**.
- The best-fit departments are not necessarily those with “simple” work, but those with:
    - digital workflows,
    - repeatable processes,
    - high text/document volume,
    - clear routing and approvals,
    - measurable outcomes,
    - manageable risk.
- **Sales** and **Product** have many AI-assist opportunities, but are weaker candidates for full AI-native transformation because their core value depends heavily on trust, judgment, negotiation, creativity, market sense, and internal politics.
- **Support Ops** scores highest on AI-native fit, but the market is highly saturated with AI support tools.
- **Procurement / Vendor Ops** emerged as a stronger opportunity than initially identified because it combines high AI-native fit with lower saturation than Support, Sales Ops, Engineering, or Legal AI.
- Procurement is not simply “FinOps”; it is better framed as **Procurement Ops / Vendor Ops / Spend Management**, usually sitting under Finance, Ops, or cross-functionally across Finance, Legal, IT/Security, and business owners.
- The negotiation part of procurement is not ideal for full automation, but is highly suitable for **AI-prepared, human-led negotiation**: briefs, benchmarks, counter-offers, concessions tracking, renewal leverage, and approval routing.

## Conclusions

- The best general “AI-native fit” metric should evaluate the **whole department**, not isolated use cases.
- By that standard, the strongest departments are:
    1. Customer Support / Support Ops
    2. Procurement / Vendor Ops
    3. Finance Ops / Billing / Collections
    4. Legal Ops / Contract Ops
- When market saturation is added, **Procurement / Vendor Ops becomes the most interesting sweet spot**.
- The strongest value proposition is not “AI procurement software,” but an **AI execution layer for vendor spend and renewals**.
- The ideal wedge is likely around **SaaS renewals and procurement execution** for mid-market tech companies: intake, approvals, usage review, renewal alerts, negotiation prep, legal/security routing, and follow-up.
- The opportunity is attractive because the pain is tied to money, time, internal coordination, vendor sprawl, and missed renewals — all measurable and business-critical.
- The main risk is competing too directly with existing procurement platforms like Zip, Coupa, Tropic, Vendr, Vertice, Spendflo, Zylo, and others.

## Hypotheses

- Mid-market tech companies with 200–1,000 employees suffer from vendor/SaaS sprawl before they have a mature procurement function.
- CFOs, VP Finance, Finance Ops, or Procurement Ops leaders may be willing buyers if the product shows clear savings and cycle-time reduction.
- A Slack/email-native AI execution layer may be easier to adopt than a full procurement suite.
- The most compelling initial wedge may be **AI renewal manager for SaaS vendors**, because ROI is concrete and the workflow is recurring.
- A “negotiation cockpit” could be valuable if it uses actual usage, contract, pricing, benchmark, renewal, and concession data.
- Pricing should likely be annual platform-based rather than pure per-seat, because the value is tied to spend controlled and savings captured.
- A realistic initial ACV could be around **$24K–$60K**, with potential to reach **$75K–$150K** if savings and workflow impact are proven.
- The product should avoid positioning itself as a full procurement suite and instead focus on execution, orchestration, and cross-functional workflow.

## Decisions

- Use **AI-native fit** as a department-level metric, not a use-case-level metric.
- Add **market saturation** as a required column when evaluating AI-native opportunities by department.
- Treat **Procurement / Vendor Ops** as the leading opportunity to analyze further.
- Frame the opportunity around **AI-native vendor spend / renewal execution**, not generic procurement automation.
- Keep procurement negotiation **human-led but AI-prepared**, rather than fully automated.
