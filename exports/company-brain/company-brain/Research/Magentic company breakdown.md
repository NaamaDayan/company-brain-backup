## **1 · The Problem They Solve**


### **Core problem**


Procurement teams at global manufacturers deal with massive document volumes, hundreds of suppliers, and ERP systems that don't talk to each other. According to McKinsey, 90% of CPOs identify supplier compliance "value leakage" as a critical issue — averaging 2% of total spend (~$40M on a $2B spend base). Identifying and fixing these gaps is done manually, with thousands of people across operative levels.


### **Secondary problems**

- Contract non-compliance: payment term errors, missing clauses, unclaimed credits
- Growing tariff risk and geopolitical supply chain complexity
- Unstructured data: incomplete master data, scattered files, unsynchronized ERPs

## **2 · The Solution**


### **What they sell**


AI agents called "Mages" — digital teammates embedded inside the procurement team. Not a dashboard, not a BI tool — they actually do the work: reading documents, emailing suppliers, updating invoices, cross-referencing ERP data. Each Mage starts with an "Internship" phase to qualify savings opportunities, then moves to full-time deployment.


### **Key capabilities**

- Reading and processing thousands of supplier documents (POs, invoices, contracts) in parallel
- Detecting contract breaches, payment errors, and unclaimed credits
- Direct supplier communication — automated emails, follow-ups, escalations
- Integration with almost any ERP and data store within days, not months
- Human-in-the-loop — Mages surface evidence and pull humans in at key review points
- Defense against supplier tariff claims

_"25% of supplier documents had issues affecting profitability — including payment terms, missing clauses, and unclaimed credits."_


— Case study, Fortune 500 manufacturer ($50B+ revenue)


## **3 · Security & Privacy**


**Compliance**


SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR — the three certifications required by Fortune 500 companies when procuring enterprise software.


**Trust architecture**


Per-customer data isolation. Customer data is never used for model training. Deterministic, policy-enforced controls over ERP access.


Access to procurement data (pricing, contracts, supplier terms) is extremely sensitive. Magentic is building trust as a core business motion — without it, their Fortune 500 ICP won't grant access.


## **4 · Go-to-Market**


### **Market entry**


They entered with proven case studies directly targeting Fortune 500 accounts: a $40B global manufacturer (4% MRO savings), a $50B+ Fortune 500 (25% document errors uncovered). The numbers are large enough to bypass typical procurement committee cycles.


### **GTM motion**

- First — free or low-cost "Internship": Mage identifies and quantifies savings opportunities
- After proof — transition to full-time engagement, payment on delivered value
- Target verticals: CPG, pharmaceuticals, advanced manufacturing
- Geography: US and Europe (offices in New York and London)

### **Pricing**


**No-cure, no-pay** — outcome-based model. Customers pay only for measurable, demonstrated value. Lowers sales friction but creates internal execution pressure at scale.

## **6 · SWOT**


**Strengths**

- Exceptional founder-market fit (McKinsey + OpenAI)
- No-cure-no-pay removes sales friction
- Sequoia lead — strong conviction signal
- Proven case studies with Fortune 500 names
- End-to-end execution, not a copilot

**Weaknesses**

- Very early company (founded 2024) — limited track record
- Fortune 500 ICP = long sales cycles, heavy bureaucracy
- No-cure-no-pay = unit economics risk at scale
- 17 employees — aggressive scalability challenge
- Narrow vertical focus (procurement/supply chain only)

**Opportunities**

- 2025 tariff wave — demand spike in progress
- Large addressable BPO displacement market
- Expansion down-market to mid-market
- Platform model — new Mages for adjacent functions
- European market still wide open

**Threats**

- Coupa and SAP Ariba embedding AI fast
- Pactum and Keelvar have more established relationships
- Fortune 500 POCs can drag on indefinitely
- Data governance regulations tightening
- Sequoia may push for aggressive hypergrowth pace

Focus: tail spend

Magentic's core differentiator: they don't provide a tool people use — they provide a teammate that does the work. Competitors still require the human to manage, approve, and navigate. Magentic executes end-to-end.


## **8 · Customers & Unit Economics**


**ICP**


Fortune 500 manufacturers: CPG, pharma, advanced manufacturing. Revenue $40B–$70B+. US and Europe.


**Proven results**


4% savings on MRO spend at a $40B manufacturer. €8.5–17M annual savings recovered in early deployments.


ROI timeline: weeks to value proof (Internship phase) → expansion across the full spend base. Strong retention mechanic — once a Mage is embedded in workflows, it becomes BAU and is nearly impossible to remove.


## **9 · Long-Term Vision**


Magentic wants to be "the AI Agent Platform for the world's supply chains." That means: additional Mages for adjacent functions (logistics, production, finance), broader geographic coverage, and ultimately a platform on which new Mages can be built and deployed — a marketplace of agents.


The core strategic bet: outcome-based pricing becomes the new default for enterprise software. If that's right, Magentic is positioned ahead of everyone else.


## **TL;DR — 6 things worth knowing**

- **Founder-market fit is as good as it gets:** McKinsey supply chain veteran + ex-OpenAI PhD. Both Oxford. That combination is not accidental.
- **No-cure-no-pay is both a sword and a shield:** it removes the sales barrier but creates real unit economics risk if execution falters at scale. Both sides are real.
- **Sequoia lead is a thesis endorsement, not just a check:** Partner Julien Bek publicly cited Magentic as an example of selling "outcomes not seats." That's a meaningful signal from a very senior person.
- **They're in our space (procurement) but strictly Fortune 500:** ICP is $40B+ revenue companies. Not mid-market. Their solution almost certainly doesn't fit $10M–$200M companies without significant rework.
- **Timing: tariffs = real demand spike:** the seed closed exactly when supply chain tension was at its peak. This is a genuine tailwind, not marketing spin.
- **17 employees, $5.5M — still very early:** the biggest risk is scale quality. Can they maintain execution fidelity as they add Mages and customers quickly?
