# Strategic Conversation Summary

**Topic:** Service as Software — Vertical Exploration & Competitive Analysis


---


## Key Insights


### Framework

- **BPO displacement > in-house tool sales.** If a task is already outsourced, there's an existing budget line, the buyer purchases outcomes, and the swap is a vendor change — not a reorg.
- **Sequoia's matrix:** Two axes — Intelligence vs. Judgement, and Outsourced vs. Insourced. Sweet spot for entry = outsourced + intelligence-heavy. Next wave = insourced + intelligence-heavy (bigger TAM, harder wedge).
- **Procurement per Sequoia is classified as insourced** — meaning mid-market procurement typically has no BPO to replace. Lio won because it targeted large enterprises that _do_ outsource procurement.
- **Three possible motions in any vertical:** (1) Replace existing BPO/outsourcer — easiest, budget exists; (2) Replace existing tool — medium friction; (3) Replace Excel/manual — hardest, no budget line.

### Competitive Landscape

- **Crowded and crossed off:** Legal AI, AP/AR, home services, property management, procurement software (Omnea, Zip, Levelpath), SaaS GTM tools, freight audit.
- **Insurance back-office:** BPO spend real and large (~$6B), Pace AI only covers enterprise, MGA mid-market underserved. High WTP. Downside: regulation, slow sales cycles, needs US-native founder.
- **Staffing back-office:** ~26,000 staffing firms in the US, BPO spend real, nearly zero AI-native competitors. Medium WTP. Has user-facing component (temp workers) which adds UI complexity.
- **Wholesale distribution:** Massive market (~$8T industry), but motion is mostly Excel replacement (no budget line). Panora (YC S24, 3 people, no follow-on funding) targets only PO entry. Distro is front-office, not back-office.
- **Procurement industrial (Tacto):** $60M raised, Sequoia + Index, targeting Mittelstand — but it's a tool, not service-as-software, and European-focused.

### New Entrants & Signals

- **Lio:** $33M raised (a16z), targets Fortune 500/Global 2000 — not mid-market. Motion = BPO displacement of outsourced procurement at enterprises.
- **HappyRobot:** $62M raised (a16z), replaces call centers in logistics/freight. BPO displacement motion. Enterprise-focused (DHL, C.H. Robinson).
- **Prysmic (Israeli):** CPG back-office tool, 3 people, replaces internal ops — not BPO displacement.
- **OpenAI DeployCo:** $4B, McKinsey/Bain as investors, targets large enterprises with Forward Deployed Engineers. Validates service-as-software motion but irrelevant to mid-market.
- **YC Summer 2026 RFS:** Explicitly requests (1) service-as-software in insurance, accounting, compliance, healthcare, and (2) "Company Brain" — executable skills file for AI agents.

### Company Brain / Skills Layer

- YC validates the problem: fragmented company knowledge needs to become an executable skills file for AI agents.
- Key distinction from Anthropic/OpenAI risk: they build better models; they don't build knowledge of _how your specific company works_. The organizational knowledge layer is defensible.
- However: post-YC RFS, this space will be crowded within months. Glean ($7.2B) already adjacent.
- As a horizontal play, WTP is unclear and sales motion is hard. As a vertical-specific play (e.g., "company brain for insurance GTM teams"), it becomes more defensible.

---


## Conclusions

1. **Service-as-software in a specific vertical remains the strongest bet** — larger TAM, clearer WTP, defensible moat via operational depth, and protection from OpenAI/Anthropic commoditization.
2. **Sequoia's first wave (outsourced + intelligence-heavy) is the right entry point.** Staffing and insurance back-office are the two remaining open verticals that fit. Procurement is classified as insourced — making it a harder wedge in mid-market.
3. **The Company Brain concept is a real problem** (YC-validated), but as a horizontal product it risks commoditization and unclear WTP. It could work as a vertical-specific wedge inside a service-as-software play.
4. **The biggest blocker isn't vertical selection — it's the domain expert.** Every strong vertical requires someone who has lived the pain. Finding this person should happen _before_ final vertical selection, not after.
5. **Staffing back-office is the most operationally open** (lowest competition, clear BPO spend, structured processes). Insurance is highest WTP but hardest to enter without a US-native founder with network.
