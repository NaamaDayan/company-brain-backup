## Key Insights

- **Keelvar category**: Keelvar is not general procurement software; it sits mainly in **advanced sourcing optimization / autonomous sourcing**, especially useful for complex sourcing events such as logistics, freight, supplier allocation, and scenario optimization.
- **Market penetration estimate**:
    - General e-sourcing / sourcing software adoption among relevant large orgs: roughly **35%–55%**.
    - Narrow autonomous / advanced sourcing tools: roughly **5%–12%**.
    - Keelvar’s share within the narrow category: estimated **5%–10% by customer count**, potentially higher in complex/high-value sourcing use cases.
- **Keelvar workflow**: Need → define sourcing event → invite suppliers → collect bids → validate → optimize supplier allocation → recommend award → approvals → supplier notification → ERP/contract handoff → performance monitoring.
- **Keelvar ICP**: Large enterprises with complex, recurring sourcing needs, especially in logistics-heavy sectors: manufacturing, CPG, pharma, automotive, retail, and industrial supply chains.
- **Procurement/finance AI-native low-hanging fruit** initially looked strongest around:
    - Procurement intake & orchestration
    - Supplier/vendor onboarding
    - AP exception resolution
    - Tail-spend / spot-buying automation
    - Contract/procurement execution
- **Reddit cross-validation** strengthened the case for:
    - Supplier onboarding execution
    - AP exceptions
    - Procurement/contract execution
    - Stakeholder chasing, approval routing, missing-docs tracking, and visibility
- The uploaded **Head of Procurement pain report** shifted the focus upward: the strongest pain is not merely executing procurement workflows faster, but **detecting and preventing rogue/maverick spend before procurement is involved too late**.
- The report’s top pains were:
    - Maverick / rogue spending
    - No real-time spend visibility
    - Stakeholders bypassing procurement or involving it too late
    - Supplier complexity and performance blind spots
    - Technology fragmentation
- A promising product framing emerged: **AI Procurement Radar** — an AI layer that detects early buying intent across Slack/email/Jira/card spend/etc., alerts procurement, and then drives intake, approvals, onboarding, contracts, and handoff.
- The uploaded **Lio company breakdown** showed that the broader vision — an **AI procurement workforce** that executes procurement work across systems — already exists in the market.
- Lio is positioned as a **virtual procurement department / AI procurement workforce**, not just a procurement copilot.
- Lio’s core wedge is **BPO/shared-service center displacement**, replacing or reducing operational procurement labor rather than simply improving procurement productivity.
- Lio targets primarily **Global 2000 / Fortune 500 / 10,000+ employee enterprises**, not SMB or light mid-market.
- Lio’s verticals include manufacturing/automotive, chemicals, pharma, financial services/insurance, postal services, transportation, medical technology, and retail.
- High-tech / SaaS was **not directly mentioned** as a core vertical in the Lio report.
- Lio’s customers are likely organizations already used to thinking in terms of outsourced/shared-service procurement labor, which makes labor-substitution ROI easier to sell.
- The report explicitly frames Lio as replacing operational procurement labor, freeing FTE-equivalent capacity, and reducing need for BPO/shared-service intervention.

## Conclusions

- Building a general **“Lio for procurement”** is probably not attractive as a new startup wedge; Lio is already strong, well-funded, differentiated, and focused on that exact vision.
- There may still be room for a company with a different segmentation strategy:
    - **Mid-market procurement operator** for companies too complex for spreadsheets but not ready for SAP Ariba/Coupa/Lio-style enterprise transformation.
    - **Pre-intake procurement radar** that detects buying intent before a formal request exists.
    - **Vertical-specific procurement AI** in areas like SaaS/shadow IT, healthcare vendor onboarding, construction subcontractor management, or financial-services vendor risk.
- The more promising direction may be applying the **Lio-like AI workforce model to another domain**, rather than competing head-on in procurement.
- Potential adjacent domains include:
    - Customer onboarding / implementation operations
    - Revenue/deal execution
    - Vendor risk / security reviews
    - Construction subcontractor lifecycle
    - Finance ops / AP exception handling
    - Legal/contract execution
- Of those, **B2B customer onboarding / implementation workforce** looked especially interesting because it has similar structural traits: cross-functional chasing, many systems, clear SLA/ROI, and measurable impact on time-to-value and churn.
- Procurement remains interesting, but the best wedge is likely **not full-cycle procurement execution**. It is more likely:
    - early intent detection,
    - mid-market lightweight control,
    - or verticalized procurement workflows.

## Hypotheses

- **H1:** Mid-market companies with 300–3,000 employees suffer enough procurement chaos to pay for a lightweight AI procurement control layer, but are underserved by Lio/Coupa/SAP-style enterprise products.
- **H2:** Procurement teams would pay for a system that detects buying intent before rogue spend occurs, because this directly addresses late involvement, maverick spend, and lack of visibility.
- **H3:** “Pre-intake procurement radar” may be a white space, but risks becoming a feature inside Lio, Zip, Omnea, Levelpath, or SAP Ariba.
- **H4:** Companies accustomed to BPO/shared-service models are more receptive to AI workforce products because the ROI can be framed as labor substitution rather than software productivity.
- **H5:** The Lio model may be more promising in another operational domain where no dominant AI-workforce vendor has yet emerged.
- **H6:** Customer onboarding / implementation operations in B2B companies may be structurally similar to procurement operations and could support a Lio-like AI workforce.
- **H7:** A verticalized wedge, such as construction subcontractor lifecycle or healthcare vendor onboarding, may be more defensible than horizontal procurement AI.
- **H8:** In procurement, the emotional buyer pain is not just efficiency; it is procurement being excluded, seen as a gatekeeper, and unable to prove strategic value.

## Decisions

- The original broad vision is recognized as **very close to Lio**.
- Do **not** treat “general AI procurement workforce” as a blue-ocean opportunity.
- Continue considering two viable paths:
    - a narrower procurement wedge, especially **pre-intake buying-intent detection** or mid-market procurement control;
    - or a **Lio-like AI workforce in another domain**, with customer onboarding / implementation operations as a leading candidate.
